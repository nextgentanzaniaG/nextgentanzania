Executive Summary — Q2 2026

Tanzania's property market continues its strong upward trajectory in Q2 2026. Zanzibar tourism assets lead performance with gross yields of 14–22%. Dar es Salaam prime residential prices rose 8.3% year-on-year. Commercial office vacancy in Masaki dropped to 4.2% — the lowest in 6 years. Land prices in growth corridors (Kigamboni, Bagamoyo) appreciated 12–18% over the last 12 months.

Land & Investment Hotspots

Where Capital Is Flowing

🌊
Bagamoyo SEZ
USD 10B port and SEZ project driving land prices up 38% since 2023. Still early-stage entry available. Risk: project timeline delays.
🏙️
Dodoma Capital
Government ministry relocations driving office and residential demand. Commercial land up 31% YoY. Bank auction opportunities available.
🌾
Iringa Highlands
European demand for avocado and macadamia farming tracts accelerating. 50-acre parcels at TZS 300–400M delivering 12–15% agri returns.
🚢
Kigamboni Bridge Zone
Post-bridge connectivity has unlocked Kigamboni. Land up 42% in 3 years with more infrastructure coming. Best value beachfront in DSM.
✈️
JNIA Airport Zone
Expansion of Julius Nyerere International Airport creating hospitality and logistics demand in surrounding areas. Industrial lease market very active.
🏝️
Pemba Island
Tanzania's hidden gem — ultra-low entry prices with Zanzibar's legal framework. 5-year outlook very positive as tourism infrastructure develops.
