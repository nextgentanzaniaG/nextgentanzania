Step-by-Step

How to Buy Property in Tanzania

Tanzania's property buying process is well-regulated and transparent. NEXT-GEN manages every step — from property identification to title deed transfer in your name.

1
Property Identification
Browse our listings or describe your requirements to NextGen AI. Our team presents shortlisted options with full documentation.
2
Due Diligence
We verify title deed, survey report, ownership history, rates clearance, and absence of encumbrances. All searches conducted at Ministry of Lands.
3
Offer & Negotiation
NEXT-GEN represents your interests in price negotiation. Agreed price is formalised in a Sale Agreement signed by both parties.
4
Deposit Payment
Typically 10–30% deposit secures the property. Paid to lawyer's escrow account — never directly to seller until transfer confirmed.
5
Legal Transfer
Transfer of title deed processed at Ministry of Lands. Stamp duty paid (typically 1–3%). Process takes 30–90 days.
6
Final Payment & Handover
Balance paid upon receipt of clean title. Keys and all documentation handed over. NEXT-GEN provides property management if required.
Legal Framework

Tanzania's Property Legal System

Tanzania has a clear land law framework. Foreign investors can legally own property through well-established mechanisms.

📜
Land Act (1999)
All land in Tanzania is owned by the State. Individuals hold Rights of Occupancy — a 33 or 66 year leasehold that is fully transferable and mortgageable.
🌍
Foreign Ownership
Foreigners may own property via Derivative Right of Occupancy — typically through TIC (Tanzania Investment Centre) registration for investments over USD 500K.
🏝️
Zanzibar Different
Zanzibar has its own land law. Foreign ownership easier — ZanInvest registration with no minimum threshold. Leases up to 99 years available.
🏢
Company Ownership
Most international investors purchase through a locally registered company (Tanzania or Zanzibar). We assist with company formation and TIC/ZanInvest registration.
💵
No Capital Controls
Tanzania has no restrictions on repatriation of investment returns, rental income, or sale proceeds. USD, EUR, and GBP freely remittable.
🏦
Mortgage Available
CRDB, NMB, and Standard Chartered offer mortgages to qualifying buyers including non-residents. Rates: 14–18% for TZS, 6–9% for USD.
Risk Management

Understanding and Mitigating Risk

Every investment carries risk. Here are the primary risks in Tanzania's property market and how NEXT-GEN helps you navigate them.

⚠️ Title Deed Disputes

Risk: Fraudulent or contested title deeds exist in Tanzania.
Mitigation: NEXT-GEN conducts Ministry of Lands verification on every listing before marketing.

⚠️ Currency Risk

Risk: TZS depreciation (avg 5%/yr vs USD).
Mitigation: USD-denominated contracts for international buyers. Capital gains typically exceed depreciation.

⚠️ Regulatory Changes

Risk: Land law amendments or foreign ownership restrictions.
Mitigation: Company and TIC/ZanInvest structures provide maximum protection under current and projected law.

⚠️ Liquidity

Risk: Tanzania's market is less liquid than developed markets.
Mitigation: NEXT-GEN's buyer database and international marketing reduces average time-on-market significantly.
